glomar.

Small radios, mostly. I build little wireless things on ESP32 — SIP, LoRa, Meshtastic, the occasional police scanner — and ship them before they're done. And a cat, allegedly.

github.com/GlomarGadaffi — about 27 of these, give or take.

Most of it is stuff I actually run in the field. Where I'm leaning on someone else's code, it says vendored. Where the data's made up, it says synthetic. Where I got bored, paused. No surprises.

Telephony a phone network on a chip you can lose in a drawer

pocket-dial ↗
A whole SIP PBX on one ESP32-S3. Aim a phone at it, dial, talk. No server in a closet somewhere.
pocket-dial-handset ↗
Push-to-talk SIP handset that registers to pocket-dial. The thing you actually hold.
pocket-dial-voicemail ↗
Store-and-forward voicemail for pocket-dial, for when nobody picks up.
resiprocate ↗
The SIP / ICE / TURN / STUN / RTP stack underneath. I didn't write this part.vendored

Wi-Fi / BLE seeing what the air is up to

wifi-ble-marauder ↗
Wi-Fi/BLE security tool in straight ESP-IDF: 5 GHz deauth, Thread/Matter/Zigbee, BLE 5. No Arduino, on purpose.
deauth-detector ↗
Notices when someone's kicking devices off 802.11, and tells the other nodes over LoRa.
wigle-wardriver ↗
Wardriver for Wigle.net. Logs Wi-Fi/BLE to SD with a GPS fix stapled to each one.
esp32s3-mgmt-scanner ↗
Passive 802.11 management-frame scanner for the ESP32-S3. Listens, doesn't transmit.
ble-sign-beacon ↗
Shouts a short message as a BLE advertisement. The whole point is that it's seen.

LoRa / RF long range, small power, weird waveforms

lora-rf-toolkit ↗
For pulling LoRa apart: frequency sweeps, I/Q capture, chirp and parameter analysis.
fsk-walkie-talkie ↗
Two of these and you've got walkie-talkies. Voice over FSK, Codec2 on an SX1262.
